## Problem

Cotton On Group operates 96 retail sites across South Africa, Botswana and Namibia. The brief was a complete network refresh: replace the incumbent Fortinet stack with Cisco Meraki, neutralise the single points of failure that had been quietly present for years, and do it without ever closing a till during trading hours. A retail rollout that interrupts trading is not a project that fails quietly - it fails on a P&L statement.

## Decision

I led delivery as the technical product manager. The shift that mattered was operating model: the incumbent programme was running waterfall against a multi-country, multi-vendor environment, which is the wrong tool for the job. I migrated the programme to an Agile cadence with two-week increments and explicit ready/done criteria, which compressed the end-to-end delivery cycle from eight weeks to five - a 37% reduction - without adding capacity. Disaster recovery was treated as a contract clause, not a phase: we wrote SLA-grade DR into the agreements with the ten-plus ISPs and equipment vendors involved. Single points of failure in the existing topology were identified and neutralised before the migration started, not during it. Roll-outs were scheduled outside trading hours per site, with the rollback path tested on every wave.

## Result

$2M+ programme delivered. 96 sites migrated across three countries. 98% on-time site delivery against the rolling schedule. Zero trading interruptions through the entire programme. The 37% delivery cycle reduction held through the back half of the rollout, which is the part where everyone else falls behind. This is the work I point to when a founder asks whether I can run real infrastructure at the size their fundraise implies.
