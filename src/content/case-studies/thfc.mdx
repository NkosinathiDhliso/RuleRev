## Problem

A South African organisation running a Corporate Social Investment programme needed to issue tax-deduction certificates that could withstand regulatory scrutiny. The existing workflow relied on manual record-keeping with no tamper-evident audit trail, putting certificate validity at risk.

## Decision

The core challenge is non-repudiation: every event in the donation lifecycle needs to be recorded so that no party can alter or delete it after the fact. I architected an immutable, append-only logging system around the workflow. Each event is written once and cryptographically chained to the previous event, so any later tampering breaks the chain. Certificates are generated from that log with verifiable references back to the source events.

The trade-off is operational: append-only means no retroactive edits. Corrections happen by issuing a new linked event, not by modifying the past. That constraint is the feature — it is what makes the certificate defensible under audit.

## Result

Every event produces a tamper-evident record from the moment it occurs. Certificates issued from the system are defensible under regulatory audit because the trail is verifiable end-to-end. Manual reconciliation is eliminated. The system is one of the cleanest examples of compliance-as-architecture I have shipped: the regulator's requirement is not a checklist bolted onto the product, it is the product.
