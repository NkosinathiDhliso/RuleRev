## What the platform needs to do

An MSSP platform is not a single product. It is an orchestration layer across four capabilities:

1. **Threat detection and alerting** - ingest logs from client environments, correlate events, surface anomalies
2. **Client dashboard** - each client sees their security posture, open incidents, compliance status
3. **Analyst workspace** - your SOC team triages alerts, escalates incidents, documents response
4. **Billing and SLA management** - monthly retainers, usage-based add-ons, SLA compliance reporting

The mistake most MSSP founders make is trying to build all four from scratch. You should not. The correct architecture is: buy the detection engine, build the orchestration and client experience.

## Recommended architecture

### Detection layer (buy, do not build)

Use Microsoft Sentinel, Wazuh (open source), or CrowdStrike Falcon Go depending on your price point. For SA SME clients, Wazuh deployed on Azure South Africa North gives you a SIEM at near-zero marginal cost per client. Sentinel is the premium play if your clients are already on Microsoft 365.

The detection layer is commoditised. Your value is not in writing YARA rules. Your value is in the response, the client relationship, and the compliance reporting.

### Orchestration layer (build this)

This is your product. A Next.js or React application backed by a PostgreSQL database, deployed on Azure South Africa North (for data residency - critical for POPIA compliance). It handles:

- **Multi-tenant client management** - each client is an isolated tenant with their own dashboard, users, and data boundary
- **Incident lifecycle** - alert ingestion from your SIEM, triage workflow, escalation rules, resolution tracking
- **Compliance reporting** - automated monthly reports showing POPIA alignment, threat landscape, remediation status
- **SLA tracking** - response time commitments, uptime guarantees, breach notification timelines

### Client portal (build this)

Your clients log in and see their security posture at a glance. Green/amber/red status. Open incidents. Last scan date. Compliance score. Monthly report downloads. This is what justifies the retainer - the client feels protected because they can see it.

### Infrastructure choices for SA

- **Azure South Africa North** for compute and data (POPIA data residency)
- **Azure AD B2C** for client authentication (enterprise-grade, multi-tenant)
- **PostgreSQL on Azure** with row-level security for tenant isolation
- **Azure Monitor + Log Analytics** for your own platform observability
- **Terraform** for infrastructure-as-code (you will need to replicate environments)

## POPIA compliance is your moat

Here is the insight most technical founders miss: your MSSP platform itself must be POPIA-compliant, and you must be able to prove it to your clients. This means:

- Data residency in South Africa (not US-East-1)
- Encryption at rest and in transit (TLS 1.3, AES-256)
- Access audit logs (who accessed what client data, when)
- Data processing agreements with every client
- Breach notification workflow (72-hour window to the Information Regulator)
- Right to deletion capability (a client leaves, their data must be purgeable)

If you can hand a prospective client a POPIA compliance pack for your own platform on the first sales call, you have already differentiated from 90% of the market.

## The business model that works

Monthly retainer per client. R8,000-R25,000/month for SMEs depending on scope. One analyst can manage 15-20 client environments using the platform you build. At R15k average and 20 clients, that is R300k MRR from a single analyst plus your platform.

The platform is the leverage. Without it, an analyst manages 5-8 clients manually. With it, they manage 20. That is the difference between a consultancy and a scalable business.
