But here is the problem most solar businesses hit between 20 and 100 installations: the operational complexity outgrows spreadsheets. Job scheduling, quoting, SLA management, maintenance contracts, client communication, compliance documentation - it all breaks at scale without a system.

This piece is for solar business owners who have proven the model and now need software to scale without hiring an operations team.

## The operational bottleneck at 20+ installations

At 5-10 installations per month, a founder can manage everything in their head plus a spreadsheet. At 20+, these things start breaking:

- **Quoting takes too long** - each quote is manually assembled from component pricing that changes weekly
- **Job scheduling conflicts** - double-booked crews, missed site visits, no visibility into crew availability
- **SLA contracts are untracked** - you sold 50 maintenance contracts but have no system to schedule the quarterly inspections
- **Client communication is reactive** - customers call asking "when is my installation?" because there is no portal
- **Compliance documentation is scattered** - COCs, SANS 10142 certificates, municipal applications in different folders
- **Cash flow is invisible** - you do not know which jobs are profitable until the accountant reconciles months later

The maintenance SLA business is where margins approach 40-45%. But you cannot sell 200 SLA contracts if you have no system to manage them. The software is the constraint on growth.

## What the platform needs to do

A solar business management platform has five core modules:

### 1. Quoting engine

- Component database with live pricing (panels, inverters, batteries, mounting, cabling)
- Site assessment template (roof type, orientation, shading, distance from DB board)
- Automated quote generation with margin calculation
- Client-facing quote with accept/reject and e-signature
- Quote-to-job conversion in one click

### 2. Job management

- Visual scheduling (calendar view of all crews, all jobs, all sites)
- Job lifecycle: quoted, accepted, scheduled, in-progress, QA, complete, invoiced
- Photo documentation at each stage (before, during, after)
- Crew assignment with skill matching (not every electrician can do battery commissioning)
- Automated client notifications at each stage change

### 3. SLA and maintenance management

- Contract database (client, system specs, SLA terms, inspection schedule)
- Automated scheduling of quarterly/biannual inspections
- Maintenance checklist templates (panel cleaning, inverter health, battery state-of-health)
- Performance monitoring integration (if the client has a monitoring system, pull production data)
- Automated alerts when a system underperforms expected output

### 4. Client portal

- Client logs in and sees: their system specs, installation photos, COC documents, next maintenance date, production data (if monitored), invoice history
- Self-service maintenance request submission
- Referral programme tracking (solar is heavily word-of-mouth)

### 5. Financial visibility

- Job costing: materials + labour + subcontractor costs vs. quoted price = actual margin per job
- Pipeline value: total quoted value, conversion rate, expected revenue
- SLA revenue: monthly recurring revenue from maintenance contracts
- Overdue invoices and payment tracking

## Build vs. buy

There are off-the-shelf options: Jobber, Tradify, ServiceM8. They work for general trades but miss solar-specific needs:

- No component database with solar pricing
- No SLA/maintenance scheduling at the level solar requires
- No municipal application tracking
- No COC document management
- No production monitoring integration
- Not hosted in South Africa (POPIA consideration for client data)

The hybrid approach: use an off-the-shelf tool for basic job scheduling, then build a custom layer on top for quoting, SLA management, and the client portal. Or build purpose-built from the start if you are scaling aggressively.

## Technical architecture for a custom build

- **Next.js** frontend (fast, SEO-friendly client portal, works on mobile for field crews)
- **PostgreSQL** on Azure South Africa North (data residency, relational data fits perfectly)
- **Azure Blob Storage** for photos and documents (COCs, quotes, invoices)
- **Twilio or WhatsApp Business API** for automated client notifications
- **Xero API** integration for invoicing and financial sync
- **PWA (Progressive Web App)** for field crews - works offline, syncs when back in coverage
